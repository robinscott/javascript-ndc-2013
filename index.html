What is User Experience?

( Often abbreviated to UX )

  • In my experience there is some confusion around what User Experience is?
  • So lets try and define it.
  • Many define UX as visual design, or other design related activities like ...

A narrow definition of UX :

The design of the user interface of a website or web app.


  • So a narrow definition might be "The user interface design of a website or web app."
  • In fact UX is much more than this.

  A broad definition of UX :

Every aspect of the user's interaction with a product, service, or company that make up the user's perceptions of the whole.

- Nielsen Norman Group (Jakob Nielsen)

  • Jakob Nielsen (the respected usability specialist) has this definition for UX ...
  • He suggests that UX is more than just the experience of using a website or mobile app, it's every interaction a user has with a product or business.
  • This also includes users within a business who use internal systems.

Why does experience matter for the website or app user?

  • So, we've looked at broad definition for UX.
  • Also how almost everyone in a business contributes towards their user's experience (even if they aren't customer facing.)
  • It certainly isn't just the responsibility of a web designer or graphic designer.
  • At Comparethemarket we're primarily a web based business so now I'll focus on website users and how UX practices can meet their needs.
  • This shows the user's hierarchy of needs when using a website or app.
  • The most important need is at the bottom of the pyramid, going up to the least important at the top.
  • It's interesting that the visual design, which is a popular definition of UX, is low on list of user's needs.
  • First and foremost the user is looking for the functionality that allows them to achieve what they want.
  • And secondly reliability so the user can trust that the functionality will be available for them to use.
  • It's important for us as web developers that we meet our user's needs because the web is a very busy and noisy environment.
  • There are apps and websites pushing notifications at the user, requesting their attention.
  • This noise can easily distract the user from their goal.
  • Also, there is a lot of choice.
  • For example, if the user's goal is to compare car insurance then they have a wide choice of comparison sites.
  • This can mean that a user will quickly abandon a website or app if their experience is too challenging and go to a competitor.

Practices and techniques
for building a successful UX

  • So let's look at a few practices and techniques that can help us reach a better UX result.
  • At CTM we try to adopt practices in our teams that will give us the best chance of providing our users with a great experience.

Core-functionality

Collaboration games

  • To meet the most important user need of core-functionality we use collaboration games.
  • Collaboration games are team exercises where everyone generates ideas for functionality and features.
  • This is how it works...

"What features and functionality do users need?"

3 minutes brainstorming
on post-its.

Post-its are stuck on the wall in random order.

  • First the team is given a focus question - "What features and functionality do users need?"
  • Next, the team write as many ideas as possible in just three minutes.
  • All ideas are stuck on the wall in random order.

Ideas are grouped and given a title.

Groups are voted for with dots.

  • Ideas are grouped and each group is given a title.
  • Then the team votes on each group based on importance to the user of that functionality.

Groups are ordered by importance.

  • Quickly generates and sorts lots of ideas
  • The whole team has input
  • Allows ideas to be quickly discarded by the team
  • Finally, groups are ordered vertically by how important the team thinks the functionality is.

Reliability

Monitoring and optimisation

  • To meet the user's need for reliability we use monitoring and optimisation to ensure reliability.
  • Monitoring allows us to watch the performance of the webite and for any service issues.
  • Also the normal activities of users on the website can be tracked closely.
  • This shows the number of user's completing the car insurance journey during different times of the day.
  • If the monitoring shows an unexpected deviation from normal user behaviour, it could indicate an issue or fault on the website.
  • Monitoring means it's possible to ensure reliabality and respond to any issues.
  • We also try to optimise reliability by improving performance - won't talk about this.

Usability and design

'Lean UX' and rapid prototyping

  • We try to follow lean UX principles and use rapid protying to develop the user interface.

Points to takeaway

  • UX is much more than just design: functionality and reliability come first.
  • Whatever you do in IT, you have users you should care about.
  • Attempt to understand your user and monitor their behaviour.
  • Get your whole team involved in generating ideas.
  • Iterate quickly and fail fast - sketch!
